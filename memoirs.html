About this work:

My work uses the visual representations of old television sets to form a memoir by splicing together digital clips and forming a cohesive narrative.

Television is a symbolic framework for my life in China, reflecting how technology and media shape our view of the past.

Through a combination of black and white video clips, Mosaic overlays and sound design, the work explores themes of identity, nostalgia and transformation.

The Mosaic effect of black lines reinforces the fragmentation and reconstruction of memory, allowing each viewer to interpret the story through their own lens. 

And it ends with an American emergency broadcast video, symbolizing a decisive shift from one stage of life to another, or a lingering regret for abandoning the past.

This is a digital memoir that explores the interplay between my personal memories of myself, loss, and finding meaning in a new cultural context.
